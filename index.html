Academic Research Summary

My research journey has been dedicated to pushing the boundaries of neuroscience and biomedical engineering, driving transformative innovations in the field of brain response analysis and physiological modeling. My work has been instrumental in several critical areas that bridge cutting-edge technology and clinical applications:

Sparse Recovery Enhancement: I significantly reduced the time complexity of sparse recovery in detecting event-related brain responses from electrodermal activity, transitioning from ~O(n^3) to a highly efficient O(n). This advancement holds tremendous potential for real-time edge computation and big data applications, employing Bayesian filters and state-space modeling techniques.

Electrodermal Activity Dynamics Modeling: I introduced a pioneering dynamical model for electrodermal activity, rooted in physiological insights, to offer comprehensive representations. This model, a product of extensive research involving physiological dynamics, statistical modeling, and state-space analysis, signifies a pivotal contribution to the biomedical research landscape.

AI Algorithm Improvements: Leveraging unsupervised machine learning frameworks like Expectation Maximization (EM), I enhanced brain activity detection algorithms, achieving an impressive AUC metric of 0.82 for the ROC from electrodermal signals, surpassing competitors by demonstrating superior accuracy.

Artifact Removal and AI Inference Enhancement: Employing advanced adaptive filtering techniques in MATLAB, I successfully removed motion artifacts from electrodermal activity. Furthermore, I optimized AI inference tools by fusing limited multi-sensor data with medical knowledge, minimizing errors to less than 7% through probabilistic modeling and optimization strategies.

Convex Optimization and Collaborative Research: I transformed non-convex optimization objectives into convex solutions using continuous system identification techniques, unraveling physiological hidden variables. Collaborating with medical professionals, I contributed significantly to characterizing hormone dysregulation in chronic fatigue syndrome and fibromyalgia patients, employing state-space modeling and sparse deconvolution methods.

Human Subject Experimentation and Publications: My extensive human subject experiments, including in-depth quantitative analyses and co-authoring publications in leading biomedical and signal processing journals and conferences, have garnered over 300 citations since 2019. These publications, with impactful placements in prestigious platforms like IEEE TBME, EMBC, and others, underscore the depth and breadth of my contributions to the field.

My research trajectory reflects a commitment to advancing the frontiers of neuroscience and biomedical engineering, merging theoretical insights with practical applications to drive impactful changes in understanding brain functionality and clinical diagnostics.
